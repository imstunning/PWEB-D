Local Foods of Surabaya

Discover the authentic taste of Surabaya through its traditional dishes

Lontong Balap

Is a food that consisting of compressed rice cakes (lontong), bean sprouts, fried tofu, lentho (black-eyed pea fritters), and a rich shrimp paste-based broth, served with fried shallots and sweet soy sauce. Its name, meaning "racing rice cake," comes from the historical practice of street vendors rushing to their posts on bicycles, creating a "race" to sell their food.

Rawon

Is a black beef soup originating from East Java, notable for its deep black color and rich, earthy taste derived from the keluak nut. The dish is made with tender cuts of beef slow-braised in a fragrant broth seasoned with spices like galangal, lemongrass, and turmeric. Rawon is often served with steamed rice, salted eggs, bean sprouts, and a spicy sambal

Tahu Tek

the dish itself consists of medium cooked fried tofu, bite sized lontong, steamed/fried potato, bean sprouts, slices of cucumber and then topped with peanut sauce. The name tahu tek derives from the sound that the scissors make when cutting the ingredients into pieces even though the cutting is done but the scissors keep on cutting and thus, it makes the sound.

Rujak Cingur

Is a food that consisting of a mix of tropical fruits and vegetables, such as young mango, pineapple, and water spinach, along with other ingredients like fried tofu, tempeh, and the unique ingredient “cingur”, which is boiled beef snout. All those components are coated in a thick, pungent, and savory black sauce made from fermented shrimp paste (petis), ground peanuts, palm sugar, and tamarind, creating a distinct sweet, spicy, and umami flavor profile.
